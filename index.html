Argument

The argument over guns and what to do about them has been a major issue in the United States for a long time. Tension is only increasing as the number of mass shootings accumulate along with the death toll. With lives lost and grieving families, people who stand for gun ownership claim it provides them security. However, it has caused more pain than helped. According to New York times, from 2000 to 2014 alone,there was on 160 mass shootings and close to five hundred deaths. This doesn't even include the Las Vegas shooting in 2017 where 58 people were left dead and 851 injured. An even more alarming problem is the suicide count due to guns. There has even been instances of children getting possession of them, due to owners irresponsibility to maintain them in secure locations. For such a developed nation and even boasting about being a leading world power, why is something like this so problematic?

Gun accessibility in America is virtually effortless, as you can attain them through various sources without much hassle. Under federal law, a person has to be 18 years old to purchase a shotgun or rifle and 21 to purchase a handgun. Also, federal law does not require a permit to purchase or own a firearm, and only 14 states passed laws to control this. Statistics

Gun Ownership

When it comes to gun ownership the United States alone, tops over all other countries with posessimg the most guns

The United States population makes up a little over 4% of the world's total population but yet we own nearly half of the guns owned by civilians world wide

About a quarter of Americans own guns according to the numbers from General Social Survey and Gallup in 2013

Deaths Caused by Guns

Here are just some of the stats of gun violence by the numbers

  • On average in a day, 96 Americans are killed with guns
  • There are nearly 13,000 gun homicides on average a year in the United States
  • For every one person killed by a gun, two more are injured
  • 62% of firearm deaths in the United States are suicides
  • America's gun homicide rate is more than 25 times the average of other developed countries

These are just some of the many other statistics that have to do with guns and the effect it has on the country and its people

Solutions

Increasing Gun Safety and Security

A way to increase gun safety is being aware of the safety rules that come with owning a gun. You should always treat a gun like it is loaded, aiming at a safe direction to avoid accidentally shooting someone or something. You also should not aim your gun at anything you don't want to destroy, and you should always be mindful of your surroundings, so you do not accidently shoot anything behind your target or trip over something.
Another way to increase gun safety are gun locks, these locks are able to be unlocked with your fingertips so it is easier to protect your friends, family, and the people around you. A company that manufactures these locks are IdentiLock.
A way to increase the security of guns is removing the rule that if an FBI background checks takes longer than three days,the gun sale is approved by default so people like Dylann Roof who killed nine people at black church in south carolina in 2015 are not able to obtain a gun and do harm.
